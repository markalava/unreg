unreg provides convenience functions for working with country and country classification schemes, and their ISO codes (United Nations 2019d). It is based on the package (United Nations 2019c), specifically the data set UNlocations in that package.

Motivation

Many demographic indicators are presented by country and geographic region. Regions are sets (or aggregates) of countries defined in a particular classification scheme, or ‘region family’. Region families in common use are the M49 (United Nations 2019d) and World Bank Income Group families (World Bank 2019).

Mappings between countries and regions used by the World Population Prospects (United Nations 2019b) are given in the UNlocations data frame in the wpp2019 package (United Nations 2019c). However, this file is quite complicated. In particular, the coding of many regions is done through specially named columns.

library(wpp2019)
data(UNlocations)
UNlocations[1:3,1:8]
#>                     name country_code reg_code reg_name area_code area_name
#> 1                  World          900       -1                 -1          
#> 2 More developed regions          901       -1                 -1          
#> 3 Less developed regions          902       -1                 -1          
#>   location_type tree_level
#> 1             0          1
#> 2        901000          3
#> 3        902000          3

unreg provides many convenience functions that make working with the country and region names and codes much easier.

Region Families

Region families are either hierarchical or binary. Several families of each kind are supported.

Hierarchical Families

Hierarchical familes have a completely nested structure. All hierarchical families have one or two levels.

M49
Two-level geographic grouping with two levels, e.g., “Africa”, “Western Africa”, … . See (United Nations 2019d).
SDG-Region
Two-level geographic grouping with two levels, e.g., “Sub-Saharan Africa”, “Western Africa”, … . These were used in a 2017 report to track progress towards the Sustainable Development Goals of the United Nations (United Nations 2019a). Following WPP 2019 (United Nations 2019c), unreg further disaggregates sub-Saharang Africa in the relevant subregions from the M49 standard.
World Bank Income Groups
One-level geographic grouping [Not yet implemented]
Legacy Development Groups
[Not yet implemented, “more developed”, “less developed”, etc.] — maybe might not be strictly hierarchical

Binary Families

Binary families have only partially nested structures, or no nesting. They are represented by binary indicator variables which include or exclude countries.

Package Operation

Function Groups

Three groups of functions are provided, each for addressing a specific task:

  1. Simple listing functions
  2. Translation functions for translating between names and codes
  3. Tabulation functions for creating easy-to-read tables of countries and regions

Listing Functions

These functions are designed to list all available country and region names and codes. Their names all begin with list_.

#### List all names---countries and regions

head(unreg::list_names())
#> [1] "World"                                                      
#> [2] "More developed regions"                                     
#> [3] "Less developed regions"                                     
#> [4] "Least developed countries"                                  
#> [5] "Less developed regions, excluding least developed countries"
#> [6] "Less developed regions, excluding China"

#### List all codes---countries and regions

head(unreg::list_codes())
#> [1] 900 901 902 941 934 948

Translation Functions

These functions translate names (countries and regions) into their ISO codes and vice-versa. They are designed to return a vector of names or codes. Their results could be looped over, for example, or for interactive use.

#### Translate a country/region code into a name
unreg::name(c(250, 903))
#> [1] "France" "Africa"

#### Go the other way
unreg::code(c("France", "Africa"))
#> [1] 250 903

#### All names are case insensitive
unreg::code(c("france", "FRanCe"))
#> [1] 250 250

#### Some regions are in two families--- specify the family
unreg::code(c("Latin America and the Caribbean", "Europe", "Northern America"))
#> Warning in unreg::code(c("Latin America and the Caribbean", "Europe", "Northern
#> America")): A 'name' has multiple 'codes' and 'family' was not specified.
#> Returning the code from the 'M49' family (the name was probably 'Europe', 'Latin
#> America and the Caribbean', or 'Nothern America').
#> [1] 904 908 905
unreg::code(c("Latin America and the Caribbean", "Europe", "Northern America"),
            family = "SDG")
#> [1] 1830  917  918
unreg::code(c("Latin America and the Caribbean", "Europe", "Northern America"),
            family = "M49")
#> [1] 904 908 905

#### You can abbreviate LAC
unreg:::code("lac", "SDG")
#> [1] 1830

If you want to get the region a country belongs to use unreg::reg_code() or unreg::reg_name(). You can supply the country as its code or its name (case insensitive).

#### Default family is "M49"
unreg::reg_code(250)
#> [1] 908
unreg::reg_code(c("France", "spAIn"))
#> [1] 908 908

unreg::reg_code(250, family = "SDG")
#> [1] 1829

#### Default level is 1
#### Give the level to get specific level of region (can be supplied as numeric for convenience)
unreg::reg_code(250, 2, family = "SDG")
#> [1] 926

#### If you want the name, instead of the code, use 'unreg::reg_name'
unreg::reg_name("france", family = "SDG")
#> [1] "Europe and Northern America"
unreg::reg_name(250, 2, family = "SDG")
#> [1] "Western Europe"

To get the mappings between countries and regions, use unreg::country_codes() and unreg::country_names().

unreg::country_codes("Africa")
#>  [1] 108 174 262 232 231 404 450 454 480 175 508 638 646 690 706 728 800 834 894
#> [20] 716  24 120 140 148 178 180 226 266 678  72 748 426 516 710 204 854 132 384
#> [39] 270 288 324 624 430 466 478 562 566 654 686 694 768  12 818 434 504 729 788
#> [58] 732

unreg::country_names("Northern America")
#> [1] "Bermuda"                   "Canada"                   
#> [3] "Greenland"                 "Saint Pierre and Miquelon"
#> [5] "United States of America"
unreg::country_names(905)       #unreg::code("Northern America", family = "M49") is 905
#> [1] "Bermuda"                   "Canada"                   
#> [3] "Greenland"                 "Saint Pierre and Miquelon"
#> [5] "United States of America"

Tabulation Functions

The tabulation functions are designed to return tables as R data frames that show countries and regions.

#### Level 1
head(unreg::reg_table(level = 1, family = "M49"))
#>   country_code country_name M49_L1_code M49_L1_name
#> 1          108      Burundi         903      Africa
#> 2          174      Comoros         903      Africa
#> 3          262     Djibouti         903      Africa
#> 4          232      Eritrea         903      Africa
#> 5          231     Ethiopia         903      Africa
#> 6          404        Kenya         903      Africa

#### Level 2
head(unreg::reg_table(level = 2, family = "M49"))
#>   country_code country_name M49_L2_code    M49_L2_name
#> 1          108      Burundi         910 Eastern Africa
#> 2          174      Comoros         910 Eastern Africa
#> 3          262     Djibouti         910 Eastern Africa
#> 4          232      Eritrea         910 Eastern Africa
